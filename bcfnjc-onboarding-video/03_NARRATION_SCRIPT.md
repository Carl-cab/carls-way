# BCFNJC IT Onboarding Video — Full Narration Script

**Tone:** Friendly, professional, calm, welcoming  
**Reading pace:** ~130 words per minute  
**Total word count:** ~1,600 words (~12 minutes of narration)  

---

## [SCENE 01 — INTRO / WELCOME]
*Avatar on screen. Warm, direct delivery.*

> Hello, and welcome to the BC First Nations Justice Council.

> My name is [Avatar Name], and I'm here to walk you through everything you need to get started with your technology at BCFNJC.

> In this video, we'll cover your laptop setup, how to care for and secure your device, how to reach IT support, and a guided tour of the Microsoft 365 tools you'll use every day.

> Whether you're brand new to Microsoft 365 or just need to get familiar with how we do things here, this video is your guide. Let's get started.

---

## [SCENE 02 — HARDWARE OVERVIEW]
*Avatar in pip. Screenshot of laptop on screen.*

> Let's start with your laptop.

> You'll notice there's a small physical privacy shutter on your webcam. Get in the habit of sliding it closed whenever you're not on a video call — it's a simple way to protect your privacy.

> For best performance, keep your laptop plugged in when you're at your desk. And whenever possible, connect using an Ethernet cable. Wired connections are faster and more stable than Wi-Fi, especially for video calls and large file transfers.

> Finally — and this one is easy to forget — please reboot your laptop at least once every few days. This keeps everything running smoothly and makes sure important security updates get installed properly.

---

## [SCENE 03 — CARE & SECURITY]
*Avatar in pip. Clean info card on screen.*

> Taking good care of your device also means taking care of the people we serve.

> When you're traveling with your laptop, keep it in a secure bag and never leave it unattended in a car or public space.

> If your device is ever lost, stolen, or damaged — even a cracked screen — please notify IT immediately. The faster we know, the faster we can protect your account and our organization's data.

> Protecting our clients' information is something we all take seriously here. Please be thoughtful about where you use your laptop and who can see your screen.

> For personal use, please keep it to a minimum and stay within our Acceptable Use Policy, which you can find on the Intranet. If you have travel coming up that might involve exceptions to standard policy, reach out to IT in advance — we're happy to help.

---

## [SCENE 04 — IT SUPPORT]
*Avatar center-screen. Contact card visible.*

> If you ever run into a technical issue, we make it easy to get help.

> Your first stop should be the Intranet — it has answers to the most common questions, along with IT forms and policies.

> If you need direct support, you can email us at support@sfy.ca, or call 1-877-378-6730. We're here Monday through Friday and we'll get back to you as quickly as possible.

> Don't hesitate to reach out — that's what we're here for.

---

## [SCENE 05 — HARDWARE REQUIREMENTS & PRINTERS]
*Avatar in pip. Icon animations on screen.*

> If you ever need additional hardware — a monitor, a keyboard, a headset — start by talking to your manager. They'll work with IT to make sure you have what you need.

> Your laptop comes with antivirus and endpoint protection software already installed. You don't need to do anything to set that up — it's running in the background keeping you protected.

> For printers: once you're connected to the staff network, reach out to SFY to have a printer deployed to your device. It typically appears in your printer list within 10 to 15 minutes. Easy.

---

## [SCENE 06 — MICROSOFT EDGE, INTRANET & SHAREPOINT]
*Avatar in pip. Edge and Intranet screenshots on screen.*

> Microsoft Edge is the browser we use at BCFNJC. Once you sign in with your Microsoft 365 account, your bookmarks, passwords, and browser settings will sync automatically across any device you use.

> Your first stop online should be the Intranet. It's your central hub for everything BCFNJC — staff directories, organizational resources, IT forms, and our Acceptable Use Policy.

> Bookmark it now so it's always one click away.

> You'll also have access to BCFNJC's SharePoint environment, where shared documents and resources are stored. We'll come back to that in the OneDrive section.

---

## [SCENE 07 — ONEDRIVE & FILE MANAGEMENT]
*Avatar in pip. File Explorer and OneDrive screenshots.*

> OneDrive is where your files live — and it's working for you automatically in the background.

> Your Desktop, Documents, and Pictures folders are all backed up to OneDrive. So if anything happens to your laptop, your files are safe in the cloud.

> You'll see a small green checkmark icon next to files and folders in File Explorer — that means they're synced and backed up. A blue sync icon means they're currently uploading.

> If you have questions about where to store specific records or documents, reach out to records@bcfnjc.com. Our records team can guide you on file management best practices.

> And when you need to access or share organizational files with colleagues, the BCFNJC SharePoint shared library is where you'll find them.

---

## [SCENE 08 — OUTLOOK EMAIL]
*Avatar in pip. Outlook screenshots.*

> Outlook is your email and calendar home base.

> When you first open it, take a few minutes to explore the address book. You'll find all BCFNJC staff listed there, along with distribution lists for teams and departments — which makes it easy to send messages to the right group of people.

> To confirm your email is set up correctly, send a quick test email. Your IT setup guide will have details on who to send it to.

> Your Outlook calendar is where you'll schedule meetings, accept meeting invites, and check colleagues' availability. Make sure to subscribe to the All Staff calendar — it's listed in the calendar sidebar — so you stay informed about organization-wide events and closures.

---

## [SCENE 09 — MICROSOFT TEAMS]
*Avatar in pip. Teams screenshots.*

> Microsoft Teams is where the day-to-day collaboration happens at BCFNJC.

> Once you've signed in, go to your settings and enable auto-launch. That way, Teams will start automatically every time you log into your computer, and you'll never miss a message or meeting notification.

> Next, head into your notification settings and make sure you have notifications turned on for the All Staff team. This keeps you connected with organization-wide updates.

> Teams is also your tool for video and audio meetings, screen sharing, and sharing files within your team channels. If you're ever unsure whether to send an email or a Teams message — Teams is usually the faster choice for quick questions or collaboration.

---

## [SCENE 10 — MICROSOFT WORD & TEMPLATES]
*Avatar in pip. Word screenshots.*

> When you create a document at BCFNJC, always start with one of our organizational templates.

> Open Microsoft Word, go to File, then New, and you'll see a section for BCFNJC templates. These are pre-formatted with our branding, fonts, and layouts — so your documents always look consistent and professional.

> Our default font is Montserrat. Please use it in all documents you create.

> When it comes to saving: always save your files to OneDrive. When you do, AutoSave activates automatically — which means Word is saving your work every few seconds. You'll see the AutoSave toggle in the top-left corner of the window.

> Before you edit any template, save a personal copy first so the original remains intact for everyone else.

---

## [SCENE 11 — ADOBE ACROBAT PRO]
*Avatar in pip. Acrobat screenshot.*

> For working with PDFs, you have Adobe Acrobat Pro installed on your device.

> You can use it to view, edit, annotate, and digitally sign PDF documents — which comes up regularly for contracts, forms, and official correspondence.

> If you're new to Acrobat, don't worry — there are resources on the Intranet to help you get familiar with the key features. And of course, IT is always here if you have questions.

---

## [SCENE 12 — CLOSING / NEXT STEPS]
*Avatar full-screen. End card fades in.*

> And that's your IT onboarding overview. You've covered a lot of ground today — from your hardware setup to Microsoft 365, file management, and collaboration tools.

> If you missed anything or want to revisit a section, this video is available anytime on the BCFNJC SharePoint intranet.

> Remember — your Intranet is your first stop for answers. And our IT team at support@sfy.ca or 1-877-378-6730 is always here when you need us.

> Welcome to BCFNJC. We're really glad you're here, and we look forward to working with you.

## Narration Notes for Voice Talent / AI Narrator

- Pace: 125–135 words per minute
- Tone: warm, clear, professional — like a knowledgeable colleague, not a corporate recording
- Pauses: add a 0.5–1 second natural pause between bullet points
- Emphasis words: "immediately" (Scene 03), "always" (Scene 10), "automatically" (Scenes 06, 07)
- Avoid: monotone delivery, rushing, overly formal phrasing
- If using AI voice (ElevenLabs, Azure Neural TTS): use a calm mid-range voice, not overly enthusiastic
- Record at 44.1 kHz stereo WAV, normalize to -14 LUFS
