# BCFNJC Onboarding Video — Avatar Presenter Strategy

---

## Purpose of the Avatar

The avatar serves as a human anchor — giving the video warmth, credibility, and a sense that someone at BCFNJC is personally welcoming the new employee. It bridges the gap between a purely screen-recorded tutorial and a fully personal message.

---

## Recommended AI Avatar Platforms

### Option 1 — Synthesia (Recommended)
- **Best for:** Enterprise, SharePoint/Microsoft ecosystem alignment
- **Why:** Professional avatars, 140+ languages, Studio-quality output, easy script-to-video workflow
- **Avatar options:** Diverse range including professional, neutral-presenting avatars
- **Plan needed:** Starter ($29/mo) or Creator ($89/mo)
- **Output:** MP4, 1080p, can export avatar-only on transparent background

### Option 2 — HeyGen
- **Best for:** Realistic avatar movement, natural lip sync
- **Why:** Best-in-class lip sync quality, custom avatar creation from photo
- **Plan needed:** Creator ($29/mo)
- **Note:** Can create a custom avatar from a real person's photo/video if BCFNJC wants a real IT rep represented

### Option 3 — D-ID
- **Best for:** Budget-friendly, quick turnaround
- **Why:** Animates a still photo into a talking head
- **Plan needed:** Lite ($6/mo for basic)
- **Use case:** If you have a real staff photo and want to create a presenter from it quickly

### Option 4 — Microsoft Azure AI (Custom Neural Voice + Presenter)
- **Best for:** Full Microsoft stack alignment
- **Why:** Stays within Microsoft ecosystem, enterprise compliance friendly
- **Complexity:** Higher technical setup required

## Avatar Selection Criteria for BCFNJC

Given BCFNJC's mandate and community values:

| Criteria | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Appearance | Choose a professional, warm, approachable avatar — gender-neutral options preferred unless a real staff member is represented |
| Attire | Business casual — blazer or smart shirt; avoid overly corporate or cold presentation |
| Background | Clean office or neutral gradient background — never distracting |
| Tone | Friendly and human — not robotic or overly formal |
| Diversity | Select an avatar that feels representative and welcoming to diverse Indigenous and non-Indigenous staff |

---

## Avatar Placement & Framing

### Full-Frame Mode (Scenes 01, 12)
- Avatar fills 60% of frame, centered or slightly left
- Clean background (blurred office or gradient)
- Lower-third name bar: "[Presenter Name] | IT Onboarding Specialist, BCFNJC"
- Used for: welcome and closing — maximum human connection moments

### Picture-in-Picture Mode (Scenes 02–11)
- Avatar occupies lower-right corner: 320×320px rounded square or circle
- Positioned so it doesn't overlap key UI elements
- Subtle drop shadow to separate avatar from screenshot background
- Avatar can gesture (nodding, pointing left toward screen) during key moments

### Side-by-Side Mode (Optional — for IT Support scene)
- Avatar left 40%, contact card right 60%
- Use when avatar is directly explaining something on the card

## Camera Framing Suggestions (for Synthesia / HeyGen settings)

- **Crop:** Chest-up / mid-shot (not full body, not extreme close-up)
- **Eye line:** Avatar looks slightly into camera at 0° or +5° — not looking up or sideways
- **Gestures:** Mild hand gestures enabled — nodding for affirmative statements
- **Expression:** Neutral-positive — calm smile, not forced enthusiasm

---

## Avatar Script Delivery Notes

- Keep sentences short (under 20 words) for best lip-sync quality
- Avoid tongue-twister combinations in AI TTS
- Pause markers: use `[pause]` or `...` in Synthesia script for natural breath pauses
- Emphasis: use CAPS sparingly for AI TTS emphasis (e.g., "NEVER leave it unattended")
- Test all phonetic edge cases: "BCFNJC" — spell out or write "BC First Nations Justice Council" in the script

---

## Intro & Outro Moments — Detailed Direction

### INTRO (Scene 01)
- Duration: ~30 seconds of avatar on-screen
- Avatar looks to camera, smiles, begins speaking
- Light fade-in on avatar after title card
- No distracting background elements

### OUTRO (Scene 12)
- Duration: ~20 seconds of avatar on-screen
- Avatar delivers closing message with warm, genuine tone
- End card slides in alongside avatar or fades in after avatar exits
- Final frame: logo + contact info, no avatar — clean brand close

---

## Backup Option — No Avatar

If avatar tools are unavailable or budget is constrained:

- Use a professional stock photo of a friendly presenter as a static "host" image
- Animate the photo with D-ID or a simple Ken Burns effect
- This creates a human anchor without requiring a full avatar subscription
- Narration can be AI-generated (ElevenLabs, Azure Neural TTS) for consistent voice quality
